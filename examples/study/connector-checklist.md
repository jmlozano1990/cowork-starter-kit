# Connector Checklist — Study Preset

Review each connector below. For each one you want to use: open Cowork Settings > Connectors > Authorize.

---

## Google Drive

- **What it enables:** Lets Cowork read papers, notes, and documents stored in your Drive folders.
- **Do you need this?** Only if you store your papers, notes, or assignments in Google Drive rather than locally.
- **Permission scope:** Cowork requests read/write access. Scope is limited to files you explicitly share or folders you grant access to — not your entire Drive by default.
- **Data boundary:** Cowork can only access files and folders you have explicitly shared or granted access to.

---

## Gmail

- **What it enables:** Lets Cowork read emails and create draft replies — useful if you get assignment feedback or correspondence from professors via email.
- **Do you need this?** Optional for most students. Useful if you want Cowork to help draft formal emails to professors or advisors.
- **Permission scope:** Cowork requests access to read messages and create drafts.
- **Data boundary:** Note: Claude creates email drafts only — it cannot send emails, even though the authorization screen mentions email permissions. Your emails are never sent without you clicking Send manually.

---

## Slack

- **What it enables:** Lets Cowork read and post to Slack channels — useful if your study group or course uses Slack.
- **Do you need this?** Only if your class or study group communicates via Slack.
- **Permission scope:** Cowork requests access to read and post in channels you select.
- **Data boundary:** Cowork only accesses channels you explicitly authorize.

---

## Google Workspace note

If your Google account is a school-issued account (school.edu), your IT admin must authorize Claude in Google Workspace Admin Console before your personal authorization will work. Contact your institution's IT help desk if you encounter authorization errors.
