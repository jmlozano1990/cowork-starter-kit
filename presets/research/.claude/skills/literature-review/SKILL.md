## Literature Review Assistant

**When to use:** When you have multiple sources and need to understand what the field says collectively, identify themes, and find gaps.

**Instructions:** Read all provided sources. Group them by theme or argument rather than by publication date. For each theme, summarize what the literature says, which sources support it, and whether the evidence is strong or contested. Produce a structured output with: (1) Introduction — state the research question or scope; (2) Themes — one section per theme, 2-4 sentences per theme summarizing the literature and citing sources; (3) Gaps — what the current literature does not address; (4) Suggested next sources — 2-3 types of sources that would fill the identified gaps. Use author-year citations throughout. If sources conflict on a point, note the disagreement rather than resolving it artificially.

**Example prompts:**

- "Conduct a literature review on [topic] using the papers in my Literature/ folder."
- "Organize these 5 papers into themes for a literature review section."
- "What gaps exist in the literature on [topic] based on my current sources?"
