# Skill: Source Analysis

**Description:** Evaluates a single source for its main claim, evidence quality, methodology, and relevance to a research question.

**When to use:** When you want to assess whether a specific paper or article is worth including in your research, or when you need a structured critique of a source.

**Instructions:** Read the provided source carefully. Produce a structured analysis with: (1) Main claim — one sentence stating the paper's central thesis; (2) Key evidence — 3-5 bullet points summarizing the evidence or arguments used; (3) Methodology — 2-3 sentences assessing whether the method is appropriate for the claim and what its limitations are; (4) Strengths and limitations — one sentence each, specific to this source; (5) Relevance — one sentence on how this source connects to the research question I have specified. If I have not specified a research question, ask before proceeding. Flag if the source is outdated, from a low-quality venue, or contradicts established consensus without strong justification.

**Example prompts:**

- "Analyze this paper: [filename or paste abstract]."
- "Is this source credible and relevant to my research on [topic]?"
- "What are the methodological strengths and limitations of this study?"
