# Skill: Research Synthesis

**Description:** Combines insights from multiple sources into a structured, cited synthesis for writing or analysis.

**When to use:** When you need to understand what a set of sources says collectively — their agreements, disagreements, and combined implications.

**Instructions:** Read all provided sources. Identify the main claim or argument of each. Find where sources agree, where they disagree, and where one source adds nuance to another. Output: (1) Overview — one paragraph on what the literature says overall; (2) Key findings — 3-5 bullet points on the most important points, each attributed to a specific source; (3) Gaps or disagreements — what the sources do not resolve or where they conflict and why. Always attribute claims to specific sources using author-year format. Do not blend sources without attribution. If a finding appears in only one source, note it as a single-source claim rather than treating it as established. End with a one-sentence synthesis statement that captures the core insight across all sources.

**Example prompts:**

- "Synthesize the papers in my Literature/ folder on [topic]."
- "What do these three papers agree on and where do they conflict?"
- "Give me a synthesis of the evidence on [research question] from my current sources."
