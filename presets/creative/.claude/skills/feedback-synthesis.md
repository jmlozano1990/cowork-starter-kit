# Skill: Feedback Synthesis

**Description:** Combines feedback from multiple sources into a clear, prioritized creative direction for the next iteration.

**When to use:** After receiving feedback from multiple reviewers, stakeholders, or collaborators — especially when feedback is mixed or contradictory.

**Instructions:** Read all provided feedback. Identify recurring themes — what do multiple reviewers mention? Also note outliers — strong reactions that only one person had but that may still be worth addressing. Separate feedback into: (1) Clear signals — themes mentioned by 2 or more reviewers, or rated as high priority; (2) Outliers — individual reactions that may or may not represent the broader response; (3) Contradictions — where reviewers disagree and why the disagreement might matter. End with a recommended direction: given the feedback, what are the 1-2 most important things to address in the next iteration, and why? Do not recommend trying to satisfy every piece of feedback — identify the core signal and make a clear recommendation.

**Example prompts:**

- "Synthesize this feedback from 3 reviewers: [paste feedback]."
- "I got conflicting feedback on [work]. What's the signal and what's the noise?"
- "What should I prioritize in the next iteration based on this feedback? [paste]."
