# Skill: Creative Brief

**Description:** Structures a vague project problem into a clear, actionable creative brief with audience, principles, and constraints.

**When to use:** When starting a new project, sharpening a client brief, or aligning a team on creative direction.

**Instructions:** Ask for: (1) the problem being solved; (2) who the work is for; (3) what success looks like; (4) any hard constraints (format, platform, budget, deadline). If any of these are missing, ask before proceeding. Then write a brief structured as: Problem statement (1-2 sentences on what needs to be solved and why it matters), Audience (who they are and what matters to them), Principles (2-4 creative principles — what the work should feel like, not just what it should say), Constraints (non-negotiables: format, length, platform, tone restrictions), What success looks like (how to evaluate the output). Keep the brief tight and specific. A brief that tries to say everything gives no creative direction.

**Example prompts:**

- "Help me write a creative brief for [project]."
- "I have a vague brief from a client — help me sharpen it: [paste brief]."
- "What principles should guide this project? Context: [describe]."
