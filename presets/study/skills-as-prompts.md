## Research Synthesis

When synthesizing research from multiple sources, follow this approach: First, identify the main claim or argument of each source. Then find where sources agree, where they disagree, and where one source adds nuance to another. Structure the output as: (1) Overview — one paragraph on what the literature says overall, (2) Key findings — 3-5 bullet points on the most important points across sources, (3) Gaps or disagreements — what the sources do not resolve or where they conflict. Always note which source a claim comes from. Do not blend sources without attribution.

**Example prompts:**

- "Synthesize the papers in my Papers/ folder on [topic]."
- "I have three papers on protein folding. What do they agree on and where do they differ?"
- "Summarize the key findings from these sources: [paste text or list files]."

## Note-Taking

When converting reading material into study notes, follow this approach: Read the source carefully. Identify the main sections or arguments. For each section, write a 2-4 sentence summary in plain language. Pull out key terms and define them. Note any examples that make the concept concrete. Output format: use headers for each section, bullet points for key terms, and a "Key takeaway" line at the end. Do not copy-paste from the source — restate in your own words.

**Example prompts:**

- "Take notes on this PDF: [filename or paste text]."
- "Read my Notes/biology-chapter3.md and turn it into a one-page study summary."
- "What are the key terms in this paper? Define each one in 1-2 sentences."

## Flashcard Generation

When generating flashcards from study material, follow this approach: Read the source and identify concepts, definitions, processes, and facts that are worth memorizing. For each item, create a Question and an Answer. Questions should test understanding, not just recall of words — ask "What does X mean in this context?" rather than "What is X?". Each Answer should be 1-3 sentences. Output format: a numbered list of Q/A pairs. Aim for 10-20 cards per topic unless specified otherwise.

**Example prompts:**

- "Generate flashcards from my notes on [topic]."
- "Turn the key terms in this chapter into Q&A flashcards."
- "I have an exam on [subject]. Create 15 flashcards covering the most important concepts."
