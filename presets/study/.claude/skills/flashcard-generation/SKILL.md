## Flashcard Generation

**When to use:** When you want to turn notes, papers, or chapter summaries into flashcards for exam prep.

**Instructions:** Read the provided study material. Identify concepts, definitions, processes, facts, and relationships worth memorizing. For each item, create a Question and an Answer. Write questions that test understanding, not just surface recall — ask "What does X mean in this context?" or "Why does Y happen?" rather than just "What is X?". Each Answer should be 1-3 sentences — complete enough to confirm understanding but short enough to review quickly. Number each flashcard. Aim for 10-20 cards per study session unless the user specifies otherwise. At the end, suggest which cards seem most important to review first based on conceptual complexity.

**Example prompts:**

- "Generate flashcards from my Notes/metabolism.md."
- "Turn the key terms in this chapter into Q&A flashcards."
- "I have an exam on [subject] in 3 days. Create 20 flashcards on the most testable concepts."
