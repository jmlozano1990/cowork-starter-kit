# Skill: Note-Taking

**Description:** Converts reading material — papers, chapters, or raw text — into organized, concise study notes.

**When to use:** After reading a paper or completing a chapter, when you want to convert dense material into notes you can review later.

**Instructions:** Read the provided source carefully. Identify the main sections or arguments. For each section, write a 2-4 sentence summary in plain language — do not copy-paste from the source, restate in your own words. Pull out key terms and define each in one sentence. Note any examples or case studies that make abstract concepts concrete. Format the output with: (1) a header for each major section, (2) bullet points for key terms with definitions, (3) a "Key takeaway" line at the end summarizing the most important insight from the entire source. Keep the total notes concise — aim for 20-30% of the original length.

**Example prompts:**

- "Take notes on this PDF: [filename]."
- "Read my Notes/biology-chapter3.md and turn it into a one-page study summary."
- "What are the key terms in this paper? Define each one in 1-2 sentences."
