# Skill: Risk Assessment

**Description:** Identifies and structures project risks with likelihood, impact, and mitigation for tracking and stakeholder communication.

**When to use:** When starting a new project, doing a project health check, or when a new issue emerges that needs to be tracked.

**Instructions:** Ask which project and at what stage it is (planning, in-flight, or approaching completion) if not already clear. Identify the top 5-7 risks. For each risk, produce: Risk (1-sentence description of what could go wrong), Likelihood (Low/Medium/High with brief reason), Impact (Low/Medium/High with brief reason), and Mitigation (1-2 sentences on what can reduce the likelihood or impact). Format as a table. After the table, highlight the 2 risks most likely to affect the project's schedule or outcome and explain why they are the priority. If the user has an existing risk register in their Active-Projects/ folder, read it first and update rather than starting from scratch — do not duplicate risks that are already tracked.

**Example prompts:**

- "What are the top risks for [project]? We're in the planning phase."
- "Update my risk register for [project] — we just discovered [new issue]."
- "I'm managing a project to [describe]. What risks should I be tracking?"
