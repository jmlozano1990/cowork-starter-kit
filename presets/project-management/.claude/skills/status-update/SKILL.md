## Status Update Writer

**When to use:** When you need to communicate project progress to stakeholders — team, executive, or client.

**Instructions:** Ask which project, what the current state is, and who the audience is (internal team, executive sponsor, or external client) if not already clear. Then produce a status update with four sections: (1) Status — one word or phrase (On Track / At Risk / Off Track) with a one-sentence explanation of why; (2) Progress — 2-3 bullet points on what was completed since the last update, specific and factual; (3) Risks — 1-3 active risks, each with a brief mitigation note, ranked by severity; (4) Next steps — 2-3 upcoming actions with owners and dates if available. Keep the total under 200 words unless instructed otherwise. Calibrate formality and detail to the audience: executive sponsors want RAG status and key risks; teams want specific progress and blockers; clients want outcomes and schedule impact.

**Example prompts:**

- "Draft a status update for [project] for my executive stakeholder."
- "Write a brief at-risk status update for [project] — the delay is due to [issue]."
- "What's the current state of my projects? Summarize each one in one status line."
