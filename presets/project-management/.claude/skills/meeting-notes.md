# Skill: Meeting Notes

**Description:** Captures and structures meeting discussions into clean decisions and action items.

**When to use:** After a meeting, when you have rough notes, a transcript, or just your memory and need to produce structured meeting notes.

**Instructions:** Ask for the meeting date, project name, and attendees if not provided. Read any notes or transcript provided. Output in four sections: (1) Date and attendees; (2) Decisions — numbered list, each decision stated as a clear, actionable sentence (what was decided, not what was discussed); (3) Action items — numbered list, each item with a specific action, the person responsible, and due date if mentioned; (4) Open questions — anything raised but not resolved. Focus on decisions and actions, not discussion. If participants went back and forth on something without reaching a conclusion, list it as an open question. Keep the total concise. If the user gives you messy bullet points or voice memo transcript, clean it up into this format without inventing decisions or action items that were not present in the source.

**Example prompts:**

- "Capture meeting notes from this transcript: [paste]."
- "I just finished a meeting on [project]. Here's what I remember: [notes]. Structure this as meeting notes."
- "What were the action items from my Meeting-Notes/ folder this week?"
