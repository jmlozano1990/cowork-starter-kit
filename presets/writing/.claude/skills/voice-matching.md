# Skill: Voice Matching

**Description:** Writes new content in the user's established voice and style, based on samples from their previous work.

**When to use:** When you need Cowork to generate content that sounds like you rather than like a generic AI output.

**Instructions:** Before writing anything, read 2-3 samples from the user's Voice-and-Style/ folder, Published/ folder, or any writing they paste directly. Identify the key patterns: sentence length (short and punchy vs. long and flowing), vocabulary level (casual vs. formal), structural habits (heavy use of subheadings? long paragraphs? numbered lists?), and tone (warm, authoritative, ironic, conversational). Then write the requested content applying those exact patterns. Do not average the style into generic "clear writing" — match the specific idiosyncrasies you observed. After writing, include one sentence noting the voice choices you made so the user can correct you if needed. If no samples are available, ask the user to paste an example before proceeding.

**Example prompts:**

- "Read my Voice-and-Style/ folder and write a 200-word intro for an article on [topic]."
- "Write a LinkedIn post in my voice about [topic]."
- "Here's an example of my writing: [paste]. Now write an opening paragraph for my piece on [topic] in the same style."
