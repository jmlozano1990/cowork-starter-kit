## Email Drafter

**When to use:** When you need to write a professional email — declining a request, following up, communicating a decision, or handling sensitive correspondence.

**Instructions:** Ask for: (1) the recipient and their relationship to the sender (internal, external client, executive, vendor); (2) the desired outcome — what should happen after reading; (3) the tone if not obvious from context. Calibrate formality to the recipient relationship. Then draft with: a clear subject line, an opening that gets to the point in the first sentence, a body with only what is necessary, and a clear call to action or next step. Keep under 200 words unless the content requires more. Avoid filler phrases. Always output the subject line separately from the body. If the first draft feels long, offer a shorter variant. For sensitive communications (difficult news, apologies, escalations), flag the tone choice and ask for confirmation before presenting the draft.

**Example prompts:**

- "Draft a professional email declining a meeting request while keeping the relationship warm."
- "Write a follow-up email to a client who hasn't responded to our proposal in two weeks."
- "Draft a direct but professional email to my team about a deadline change."
