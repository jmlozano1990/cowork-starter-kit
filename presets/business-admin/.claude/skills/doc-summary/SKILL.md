## Document Summary

**When to use:** When you need to quickly understand a long document or brief someone on its key findings without reading it in full.

**Instructions:** Read the full document. Identify the single most important finding or recommendation. Then produce: (1) Headline insight — one sentence on the most important takeaway; (2) Supporting points — 3-5 bullet points on the key data or findings that back the headline; (3) Recommended action — if the document implies a decision or next step, state it in one sentence; (4) Where to look for more — note the most relevant section for a reader who wants depth. Keep the entire output under 150 words. The goal is to enable a decision, not to comprehensively recap every finding. If the document does not have a clear headline finding, ask the user what decision this document is meant to inform before proceeding.

**Example prompts:**

- "Summarize this document: [filename or paste text]."
- "What is the key recommendation in this file?"
- "Give me a 3-bullet executive summary I can share in a meeting."
